What are the aims of the module?

  • Refresh and develop the knowledge and skills gained from undergraduate research teaching.

  • Develop a critical understanding of research methods.

  • Develop skills in qualitative and quantitative forms of analysis.

  • Designed to explicitly prepare you for further research activity (e.g., small scale projects and doctoral thesis).

  • Please see module handbook for more information.

What will we be doing?

  • Some of the sessions are focused on qualitative research

  • Some are on quantitative research

  • Some are focused on thesis development

Using R for statistical analysis

Why learn R?

  • Free and open source
  • Widely used in academia and in the NHS
  • Powerful and flexible

Benefits of learning R

  • It separates the analysis and interpretation from a single software package

  • Your code is reproducible and can be shared with others

  • It forces you to understand what you are doing (if you work through the code)

R software is already installed on the university machines, and will be available in the labs that the sessions take place in. You can download R on your personal machine too.

You can also use Posit cloud (not for confidential data)

Approaching to research methods in the programme

Year 1

The quantitative portion of the module is built around some key concepts:

  • Designing research that examines why things happen, not just what happens

  • Understanding the importance of robust design and sample size in clinical research

  • Applying a modelling approach to analysis to answer these types of questions

How the classes will work

  • Each session will have a lecture component and a practical component

  • The practical component will outline a specific analysis approach

  • There is some reading that is relevant to each session (on BB)

  • There is an outline of some key R skills that we will use in each session (on BB)

Some things to consider

  • Limitations in research design come primarily from the conceptualisation of the research question

  • If you have developed a clear research question, the analysis should be clear

  • You will not use all the techniques we cover, but understanding them will help you understand the literature and design your own research
